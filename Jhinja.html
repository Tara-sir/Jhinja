About Jhinja

Jhinja is a serene and beautiful village nestled in the hills of Western Rukum, one of Nepal's 77 districts. With approximately 50 houses and a population of more than 200 people, the village offers a peaceful rural life while being surprisingly well-connected to neighboring towns and cities.

This small yet lively village is a part of the Sitalpokhari-Jhulkhet-Chunbang Highway, providing an important link to the Rolpa district and beyond. Jhinja is located:

  • 5 km northeast of the Mid-Hill Highway
  • 5 km from the Rapti Highway
  • 12 km from Salley Airport
  • 8 km from Musikot Khalanga, the district headquarters

Despite being a small village, Jhinja plays a crucial role as a business hub for neighboring villages like Sankh, Chaukhabang, and Bhalakcha.

Life and People of Jhinja

The village is home to a close-knit community, primarily composed of people from the Chhetri and Bahun ethnic groups. The community is actively involved in both agriculture and small-scale trade, with local markets thriving due to its connection to surrounding villages.

Education and Infrastructure

Jhinja is proud to house the Shree Bal Kalyan Higher Secondary School, which provides education to children from Jhinja and surrounding villages. The village is also powered by its own community-run Sankh Khola Hydropower Project, ensuring that every household has access to electricity, an essential aspect of its development.

Cultural Heritage of Jhinja

Jhinja is rich in cultural traditions, and one of its most celebrated cultural expressions is the Lok Pauranik Paiseru Naach. This traditional dance is performed by the पौराणिक लोक नृत्य सास्कृतिक समूह (Pauranik Lok Nritya Sanskritik Samuha), a local cultural club dedicated to preserving the region’s folk traditions. Visitors to Jhinja are often captivated by the vibrant dance performances that reflect the village’s cultural history.
